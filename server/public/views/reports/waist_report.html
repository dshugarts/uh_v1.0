Recently established as an independent risk factor for development of cardiovascular disease (CVD), it is the measurement of ones waist circumference. According to the National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute (NHLBI) waist circumference is linked to metabolic disorders such as type II diabetes, stroke, and heart diseases. In agreement, the Human Health Services (HHS) in a manuscript published at the National Institute of Health (NIH) provided evidence to the strong predictive power of waist size in predicting incidence of CVDs and found that larger circumferences significantly increase the occurrence of heart problems.
